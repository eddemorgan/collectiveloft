The problem

Every platform built "for" creatives
treats them as something else.

Service workers on Upwork. Content producers on Instagram. Static portfolio holders on Behance. Generic professionals on LinkedIn.

None of them are built for what actually drives creative output: the relationship between complementary disciplines that produces work neither party could make alone.

The creative class has never had a professional home. That changes.

LinkedIn
Built for corporate professionals. Treats a musician's profile like a résumé.
Upwork
Client-worker hierarchy. Race to the bottom on price. Not a creative relationship.
Behance
Work sits waiting to be found. Passive. No path from profile to project.
Instagram
Optimizes for attention. Engagement is the metric. Not completed work.

The platform

From the first moment of discovery
to the final handoff of finished work.

01
Creative Profiles
A professional identity built around your discipline, not a CV. Your "Right Now" card tells collaborators exactly what you're making and what you need, today.
02
Collab Briefs
Post what you're making and who you need, in creative language. Not a job listing. A conversation starter with a track record attached.
03
Discipline Matching
An algorithm that surfaces the right collaborator, with a score and the specific reasons behind it. 94% match means something here.
04
The Loft Studio
A shared workspace that opens the moment two creatives agree to work together. Brief pinned at the top. Milestones, files, chat, all in one place.
05
Collab Terms
Creative exchange, paid, or revenue share. Rights timestamped. Deliverables locked. Milestone payments only release when both parties confirm. No surprises.
